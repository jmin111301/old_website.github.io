Hi! Thanks for stopping my page!

My name is Young Woong (Jonathan) Min and I'm currently a researcher at Harvard Business School, working with Professor David Scharfstein and Luis Viceira. Before this, I spent my time at UC Berkeley, studying Statistics and Applied Mathematics. Some of my interests include computer science theory, machine learning, mathematical analysis and topology, causal inference, and Bayesian methods. In the future, I hope to leverage my skills to work as a research scientist or professor, understanding data beyond its numerical values and engineering innovative methods that can be broadly utilized in a variety of fields. On my free time, I enjoy completing projects, working out, learning new languages, finding new music, and playing basketball and baseball with friends.

My time in university was a wild journey. Initially, I was set on law school, spending most of my time reading law review articles and history books. Around my sophomore year of university, I was instead drawn to how social scientists determined the impact of policies through statistical methods. I couldn’t help but question how researchers and analysts drew conclusions. To address this, I pivoted into mathematics and statistics, building the quantitative skills necessary to be an effective statistician. This website is a product of this curiosity! I hope that the contents of this website prove useful (or at the very least are interesting). Once again, thanks for stopping by and stay in tune for more!
