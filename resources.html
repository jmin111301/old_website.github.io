Statistics

General Statistics: the standard text for a second course in undergraduate statistics is John A. Rice's Mathematical Statistics and Data Analysis. I read through most of the textbook while taking Stat 135 at Berkeley. The textbook is quite comprehensive along with a review of probability theory in the first half.

Introductory Machine Learning: I have found these two books very helpful with understanding ML concepts at a surface level. First is An Introduction to Statistical Learning: with Applications in R. Second is The Elements of Statistical Learning: Data Mining, Inference, and Prediction. I still working through the first text and will get to the second. I am enjoying the first text and its applications.

R: The most helpful resource for me has been the standard R for Data Science. I read through this text when I needed to review my R skills. I will say that this is one of the best primers of the tidyverse package!
